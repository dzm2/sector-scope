Team season assessments After round 2 · China
MERCEDESStrong
Dominant through the opening two rounds. Back-to-back 1-2 finishes in Australia and China have put them nearly 100 points clear — a stunning start to the new regulation era. Russell and Antonelli are pulling away before most rivals have found their footing.
FERRARICompetitive
The only team able to stay in sight of Mercedes. Hamilton's first Ferrari podium in China and Leclerc's consistent pace suggest the Scuderia have a genuine challenger — but they need to close a 31-point gap before the season escapes them.
MCLARENStruggling
A catastrophic start. Both cars failed to start in China after power unit problems, and Piastri also missed Australia. Stuck on 18 points after two rounds, already 80 behind Mercedes. The reigning champions need answers urgently.
HAASMidfield
The surprise package of the early season. Bearman has been exceptional — a fifth in China moved them above Red Bull in the standings. Ocon is yet to score but the pace is there. A genuine midfield contender if they can maintain this level.
RED BULLMidfield
A deeply difficult start for the most dominant team of the recent era. Two retirements in two grands prix for Verstappen, with Hadjar also crashing out in Australia. Just 12 points on the board. The new regulations have not been kind to them yet.
RACING BULLSMidfield
Quietly solid. Lawson scored in both the sprint and the race in China, and they sit level on points with Red Bull. A more consistent weekend-to-weekend team than their senior partner so far, which will make for interesting internal politics.
ALPINEMidfield
Gasly delivered a points finish in China with a sixth place, putting Alpine in a respectable position in the early standings. Under Briatore's renewed leadership, there are signs of stability — but they need both cars scoring consistently to push higher.
WILLIAMSStruggling
A painful start — Albon failed to start in China with hydraulic issues and the team has just 2 points to show. Colapinto grabbed a point in China which offered some relief. The pace isn't there yet and they need reliability before anything else.
AUDIStruggling
Two rounds in and still searching for a points finish. Bortoleto failed to start in China with hydraulic problems and Hülkenberg has been unable to convert pace into results. A transitional team in a transition year — patience is required.
ASTON MARTINBackmarker
Zero points after two races. Alonso and Stroll are yet to threaten the top ten. The team that challenged for race wins in 2023 looks a long way from that form under the new regulations. A tough season lies ahead unless development comes quickly.
CADILLACBackmarker
The new American entry faces the steep learning curve expected of a first-year team. Zero points, and Bottas and Pérez are finding life tough at the back. But making the grid and racing cleanly is itself an achievement in year one.
